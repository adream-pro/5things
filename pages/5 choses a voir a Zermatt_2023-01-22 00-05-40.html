5 choses a voir a Zermatt

Matterhorn

The Matterhorn, also known as the Monte Cervino, is one of the most iconic and recognizable peaks in the Swiss Alps. It stands at a height of 4,478 meters and is a popular destination for climbers and hikers. Visitors can take a cable car to the top for stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Gornergrat

Gornergrat is a mountain located in the Pennine Alps and offers an incredible view of the Matterhorn, as well as the Monte Rosa, Lyskamm, Castor, Pollux and many other peaks. The Gornergrat Bahn is one of the oldest mountain railways in the world, and it takes visitors to an altitude of 3,089 meters above sea level.

Zermatt Ski Resort

Zermatt Ski Resort is one of the most famous ski resorts in the world. It offers ski runs for all levels of skiers and snowboarders, as well as a variety of other winter sports activities. The resort is also home to the world-renowned Matterhorn Glacier Paradise, the highest cable car station in Europe, which offers panoramic views of the Matterhorn and the surrounding mountains.

Zermatt-Matterhorn Museum

This museum tells the story of the Matterhorn, from its geology and natural history to the early attempts to climb it and the tragic events that have occurred on its slopes. Visitors can see artifacts, photographs, and films that document the history of the Matterhorn and the people who have been drawn to it.

The Church of St. Mauritius

The Church of St. Mauritius is a beautiful, historic church located in the center of Zermatt. It was built in the 14th century and is known for its intricate frescoes and stained glass windows. The church also offers panoramic views of the Matterhorn and the surrounding mountains. Visitors can attend services or just admire the architecture.
