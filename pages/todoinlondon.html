5 things to do in London

Visit Buckingham Palace

Not only can you watch the Changing of the Guard ceremony, but you can also see the palace's beautiful gardens, which are open to the public during the summer months. Additionally, during the summer season, the palace's State Rooms are also open to visitors, allowing them to see the opulent interiors and artwork of the palace.

See a show in the West End

The West End is known for its world-class theatres, and there's always something for everyone. From classic musicals like "The Phantom of the Opera" to newer productions like "Come From Away", you can find a show that will suit your taste. Additionally, you can also check out smaller venues such as the Old Vic or the Young Vic for a more experimental theatre experience.

Explore the British Museum

The British Museum is not only a great place to learn about history and culture but also to appreciate art and architecture. The museum's Great Court, designed by architect Norman Foster, is a beautiful space to relax and enjoy a cup of coffee. Additionally, the museum offers a wide range of special exhibitions throughout the year, so make sure to check the schedule before your visit.

Take a ride on the London Eye

The London Eye is not only a great way to see the city from above, but also a great way to experience the city's lights at night. You can also take a VIP ride in one of the Eye's glass capsules for a more intimate experience.

Visit the Tower of London

The Tower of London is not only a great place to learn about the city's history but also to see the Crown Jewels. You can also take a tour with one of the Yeoman Warders, also known as "Beefeaters", to hear stories and legends about the tower. Additionally, you can also visit the White Tower, which houses the Royal Armouries, and learn about the history of weapons and armor.
