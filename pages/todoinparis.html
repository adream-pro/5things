5 things to do in Paris

Visit the Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world and a must-see for anyone visiting Paris. The tower was built for the 1889 Exposition Universelle, a world's fair celebrating the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution. You can take the elevator or climb the stairs to the top for panoramic views of the city. At the top, you can see the city's famous landmarks such as Notre-Dame Cathedral, the Louvre Museum, and the Arc de Triomphe. Visitors can also enjoy a meal at one of the tower's restaurants or sip on champagne while enjoying the view.

See the Mona Lisa at the Louvre

The Louvre is one of the world's most famous museums and is home to thousands of works of art, including the Mona Lisa. The painting is considered one of the most famous artworks in the world, and it is believed to be a portrait of Lisa Gherardini, a wealthy merchant's wife. The Louvre also houses many other famous artworks such as the Winged Victory of Samothrace, the Venus de Milo, and the Death of the Virgin. Visitors can take a guided tour of the museum or explore it on their own, taking in the many different galleries and exhibitions.

Take a stroll along the Champs-Élysées

The Champs-Élysées is a famous avenue in Paris that stretches from the Arc de Triomphe to the Place de la Concorde. The avenue is known for its luxury shops, theaters, and cafes. Visitors can stroll along the avenue, taking in the sights and sounds of the city, or stop for a cup of coffee or a meal at one of the many cafes. The avenue is also home to the Grand Palais, an exhibition hall and museum, and the Petit Palais, an art museum.

Visit Notre-Dame Cathedral

Notre-Dame Cathedral is a famous Gothic cathedral that is a masterpiece of medieval architecture. The cathedral was built in the 12th and 13th centuries and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can tour the cathedral and admire its stunning stained-glass windows, intricate carvings, and famous bell towers. The cathedral is also home to many religious artifacts, including the Crown of Thorns, believed to have been worn by Jesus during the Crucifixion.

Take a boat tour on the Seine River

A boat tour on the Seine River is a great way to see many of Paris's famous landmarks, including the Eiffel Tower, Notre-Dame, and the Louvre, from a different perspective. Visitors can take a leisurely cruise along the river, taking in the sights and sounds of the city, or opt for a guided tour that provides more information about the landmarks. The boat tour is a lovely way to enjoy the city and get a different perspective on it.
